The Royal Museum Gallery

Bust of Sir Hans Sloane

Hans Sloane (1660-1753), whose collection was purchased for the public to found the British Museum, acquired objects and natural specimens during his time in Jamaica as physician to the British Governor of the island.

The Rosetta Vase

Made by the British artist Grayson Perry for his exhibition at the British Museum 'The Tomb of the Unknown Craftsman', the vase depicts the Museum as a modern-day secular place of pilgrimage where cultures and ideas meet.

the holy thorn reliquary

This splendid reliquary was made in Paris about 1400 to display a thorn from the crown allegedly worn by Jesus Christ at his crucifixion.

The Rosetta stone

The Rosetta Stone carries a decree issued in honour of King Ptolemy V Epiphanes. It's inscribed in Egyptian hieroglyphs (favoured for monuments), in Demotic (a cursive script for daily use), and in Greek.

This salt cellar is made of ivory and represents Europeans, shown with long hair, beards and hooked noses. It was made by a master craftsperson in the Kingdom of Benin, Nigeria between 1525 – 1600. Africans along the continent's west coast first encountered Portuguese traders in the mid-15th century. This marked the beginning of regular contact and trade, during which the purchase and commissioning of carved ivory goods by Europeans became common.
